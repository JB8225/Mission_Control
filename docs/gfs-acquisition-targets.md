# GFS M&A Acquisition Targets — DFW Fire Protection

## Strategy
Acquire small-to-mid DFW fire protection/inspection companies to:
1. Instantly add ITM recurring revenue
2. Absorb their contract book into GFS
3. Cross-sell GFS construction/install to their existing customers
4. Reach $75M combined revenue over 10 years

## Acquisition Criteria (Target Profile)
- Revenue: $500K–$8M
- Heavy on inspection/monitoring (recurring revenue)
- Owner-operated, founder approaching retirement
- DFW metro or adjacent (Austin, Houston next phase)
- Price: 2-3x ARR for inspection-only, 4-5x EBITDA for full-service
- Prefer no/low debt, clean books

## Competitors to Watch (PE-backed — don't compete, watch for territory openings)
- **Summit Fire & Security** — acquired Texas Fire & Sound (Garland, FW, Houston), June 2025
- **Pye-Barker** — 20+ Texas locations after Fortress Security acquisition, Sept 2025
- **Impact Fire Services** — DFW location, national rollup
- **Automatic Fire Protection (AFP)** — acquired Apex Fire Protection, Nov 2025

*These PE rollups are creating openings: when they absorb a local company, customer relationships often get disrupted. That's a sales opportunity for GFS.*

## Acquisition Playbook
1. **Year 1 post-close:** Identify top 3 targets, begin relationship building
2. **Year 2:** Pursue first acquisition (Tier 1, $500K-$2M revenue)
3. **Year 3-4:** Second acquisition if first integration successful
4. **Year 5:** Refinance based on $25M+ valuation, cash out investors + Woody, retain equity
5. **Year 7-10:** Third acquisition or geographic expansion (Austin/Houston), final exit

## Valuation Milestones
- **Refi trigger:** $25M company valuation (cash out investors + Woody, JB stays)
- **Exit target:** Year 7-10 at $75M+ revenue, 20%+ ITM → 7-8x EBITDA = $50-100M+

- Many of these founders are in their 50s-60s with no succession plan
- The PE rollup wave (Pye-Barker, Summit, AFP) is creating urgency for independents to decide
