Resolved Family is pre-launch. The product is built. One engineering blocker (webhook split) stands between JB and revenue. Priority #1 for the company. Goal: $5K/month MRR at $49/sale (~102 sales/month). Primary growth channel: Meta ads.

## ANALYST INPUTS

---

### 🔴 MAVERICK — Growth & Revenue

**"You're leaving money on the table. Here's where."**

**Idea 1: Price anchor — offer a $99 "family plan" at checkout**
Right now you have one price: $49. That's leaving money on the table with buyers who would pay more. Add a second SKU at checkout: "The Resolved Brief — Family Plan" ($99) that includes two Briefs and a shared family vault page. A meaningful % of buyers will upgrade. Zero extra product to build — just a second Claude generation for spouse/partner. Estimated lift: 15–20% revenue increase with no new traffic.

**Idea 2: Scorecard-to-sale conversion optimization**
The scorechart is your highest-leverage conversion point. Most funnels like this lose 90%+ between scorecard completion and purchase. Before spending money on Meta ads, know your current conversion rate at every step. If you don't know it, you can't fix it. Supabase has this data — track it before launch. Target: get from scorecard → sale above 5% before scaling.

**Idea 3: Add urgency to the email drip**
Email drips without urgency die in inboxes. Add a "72-hour window" mechanic to the drip: after their free report card, they get a limited-time offer (FRIEND50 code, framed as expiring). You already have the code — use it as the close mechanism in email 3 or 4 of the drip sequence. This is free to implement and will pull sales forward.

---

### 🔵 ATLAS — Strategy & Competitive Intelligence

**"Here's what the market is doing."**

**Idea 1: Position against estate planning, not against nothing**
Right now Resolved is positioned as a preparedness tool. That's correct emotionally, but it's a small category. The real competitive frame is: *you don't need a $3,000 estate attorney to get your family prepared*. Trust & Will charges $199 for a basic will. Estate planning attorneys charge $1,500–5,000+. Resolved does in 30 minutes what takes attorneys weeks, for $49. That comparison should be in every ad and on the sales page. You're not a "preparedness doc" — you're democratized estate readiness.

**Idea 2: The "after I'm gone" market is bigger than the "just in case" market**
Current positioning leans toward crisis preparedness ("what if something happens"). But the strongest emotional trigger is post-death chaos — kids can't find the will, the bank accounts, the passwords. That's the story that converts. Lead with the negative: "Most families spend 6–18 months in chaos after losing someone because they didn't have a Resolved Brief." The market here is: people who just lost a parent to chaos and vowed not to do that to their kids.

**Idea 3: Referral flywheel is your secret weapon post-launch**
FRIEND50 is already built. The highest-converting Resolved customer is someone who just finished their Brief and feels relieved. That's the moment to ask for a referral. Add an in-app share moment right after PDF delivery: "Give your kids, your siblings, or your parents the same peace of mind — here's your referral link." This is organic distribution that costs nothing and compounds.

---

### 🟢 FORGE — Product & Technology

**"What can we build that creates a moat?"**

**Idea 1: The webhook fix IS the launch — prioritize it above everything**
This isn't a nice-to-have. It's the only thing between JB and revenue. The fix is one Claude Code session: redirect SamCart webhook → set `session_paid = true` in Supabase → send access email → on walkthrough completion button click, call `/api/session/{id}/generate-brief`. Total dev time: 2–4 hours. This is the only technical work that matters right now. Everything else is premature optimization.

**Idea 2: Add a progress indicator to the walkthrough**
A 59-question walkthrough is daunting. Completion rates will suffer without a progress bar. Add a simple "Section 2 of 6 — Money & Assets" header with a visual progress bar. Estimated lift on walkthrough completion rate: 20–30%. If users don't finish, the PDF doesn't generate, the affiliate revenue doesn't trigger, and word of mouth dies. This is a 30-minute build.

**Idea 3: "Update your Brief" reminder at 12 months**
Every Resolved customer is a future subscriber. At 12 months post-purchase, send an automated email: "Life changes. Your Resolved Brief should too." Offer the annual update at $29 (vs. $49 repurchase). This is trivially easy to build in Supabase (created_at + 365 days) and creates recurring revenue from existing customers. Builds toward eventual subscription model without requiring a subscription rebuild now.

### 🟡 LEDGER — Finance & Risk

**"Do the numbers actually work?"**

**Idea 1: Know your CAC before spending one dollar on Meta**
At $49/sale, $5K/month = 102 sales. If Meta CAC comes in at $20, that's $2,040 in ad spend for $5K revenue — solid. If CAC is $40, you're spending $4,080 for $5K revenue — barely worth it. If CAC is $60+, you're losing money. JB's fear of paid ads is rational — he's never seen return from them. The answer is not to avoid ads — it's to run a $200 test first. 4–5 days, 3 ad creatives, cold audience. You'll have your CAC signal before committing real budget. This de-risks everything.

**Idea 2: Affiliate revenue changes the unit economics**
Trust & Will pays ~$60–100 per referred will. Ethos pays $20–50 per life insurance quote. 1Password pays ~$5–15 per signup. Google One pays ~$2–5. If even 20% of Resolved buyers click through and convert on one affiliate offer, average affiliate revenue per customer could be $8–15. That drops effective CAC threshold meaningfully. At $49 + $12 affiliate = $61 effective LTV. Model this before you set your bid caps on Meta — it changes the math.

**Idea 3: The annual update subscription is your MRR path**
$5K/month from one-time $49 sales requires constant new customer acquisition. That's a treadmill. Even a small % on annual plans changes the model. 100 customers on $29/year annual = $2,900/year recurring. 500 customers = $14,500/year baseline. This isn't buildable at launch, but it should be on the roadmap for month 3. Once you have 50 customers, run a beta offer for the annual plan.

### 🟣 COMPASS — Operations & Execution

**"Great idea. Who's doing it and by when?"**

**Idea 1: Sequence matters — do NOT run ads before the webhook is fixed**
If someone buys and doesn't get access, they chargeback. They tell their friends. Trust & Will and similar products have been destroyed by bad post-purchase experiences. The webhook fix is the gate. Nothing else ships before it.

**Idea 2: Beta cohort before ads — 20 free users, get the data**
The plan was always to get 20–50 beta users from entrepreneur groups before paid launch. This is the right call. You need: completion rate, time-to-complete, NPS, and the first 3 social/referral shares. Budget: $0. Timeline: 1 week. Output: real conversion data AND testimonials for the sales page and ad creatives. Ads without social proof are dead on arrival in 2026.

**Idea 3: Meta ads need creative, not budget**
JB's fear of ads is actually a creative problem, not a budget problem. Most small businesses fail at Meta because they run corporate-looking images. Resolved's strongest ad creative will be: a UGC-style video testimonial ("I just finished mine and I can't believe I didn't do this sooner"), or a scroll-stopper stat ("63% of families can't find a will within 30 days of losing a parent"). These cost $0 to test with a $200 budget. First week of Meta: test 3 hooks, find the winner, then scale.

## THE DEBATE

**Maverick vs. Ledger — On when to run ads:**
Maverick says run a $200 test immediately post-launch to get CAC data.
Ledger agrees but adds a hard condition: not before beta cohort validates completion rate. If the product breaks in beta, a $200 ad test produces garbage data AND potential chargebacks. **Ledger wins this one.** Sequence: webhook fix → beta cohort (20 users) → $200 ad test → scale.

**Atlas vs. Forge — On positioning:**
Atlas wants to reframe Resolved as "estate planning alternative" immediately, on the sales page and in ads. Forge says the product positioning is fine — just fix the completion rate problem first. **Atlas wins long-term, Forge wins short-term.** Update the sales page positioning while building the webhook fix — zero additional dev time required.

**Compass vs. Maverick — On the family plan upsell:**
Compass says don't add complexity at launch. Maverick says it's a SamCart order bump — 20 minutes to add, no dev work. **Maverick wins.** SamCart order bumps are trivial. Add it at launch.
