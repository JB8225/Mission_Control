## HOW IT WORKS

### Layer 1: Wyoming HoldCo (The Shield)
- JB is the sole member of the Wyoming LLC
- Wyoming LLC is the **member-manager** of each property-state LLC
- Wyoming public records do NOT list JB's name — only the WY LLC appears
- This is the privacy + charging-order protection layer
- Should **not** directly own or manage properties (liability contamination risk)

### Layer 2: Property Operating LLCs (The Containers)
- One LLC per property ("one property, one LLC" — Coons' cardinal rule)
- Formed in the **state where the property physically sits**
- If property is in TX, form a Texas LLC. If in CO, a Colorado LLC.
- The Wyoming HoldCo appears as the member of each operating LLC
- Property title is deeded into the operating LLC

### Money Flow
```
Tenant Rent → Property LLC → Wyoming HoldCo → JB (personal distributions)
```

## KEY BENEFITS

### 🛡️ Asset Protection
- If a tenant sues over Property #1, the lawsuit is contained to LLC #1
- Property #2 is completely insulated — different legal entity
- Personal assets (home, savings, other investments) are protected from property-level lawsuits

### 🔒 Privacy / Anonymity
- Wyoming does not disclose member/manager names in public filings
- A potential plaintiff searching property records sees the WY LLC, not JB Bouck
- Reduces "jackpot litigation" targeting — harder to find, harder to sue
- Land trusts can be layered on top for states with weak title privacy (optional)

### ⚖️ Charging Order Protection
- Wyoming has some of the strongest charging order protection in the US
- If a creditor gets a judgment **against JB personally**, they cannot force distributions from the WY LLC or seize its assets
- Creditor can only get a charging order (right to receive distributions IF made — but JB controls that)

### 💰 Tax Efficiency
- Wyoming has **no state income tax**
- Single-member LLCs are "disregarded entities" for federal tax — income flows to JB's personal return
- No additional federal tax complexity for a 2-property structure
- Each property LLC can make its own elections as needed

### 🏦 Financing Friendliness
- Some lenders will lend to LLCs for investment property (especially portfolio/commercial lenders)
- Structure is clean and well-documented — easier for future lenders or investors to review

## SETUP STEPS

### Step 1: Form Wyoming HoldCo LLC
- File Articles of Organization with Wyoming Secretary of State
- Choose a generic name (not geographically identifying) — e.g., "One Buck Holdings WY, LLC"
- Appoint a registered agent in Wyoming (required)
- **Do NOT use your personal name/address** in public filings

### Step 2: Draft Operating Agreement for WY LLC
- Written operating agreement naming JB as sole member
- Specifies JB's management rights and distribution rules
- Not filed with state — stays internal

### Step 3: Form Property-State LLC(s)
- Form one LLC in the state of each rental property
- Name them something non-descriptive (e.g., "OBC Property 1, LLC")
- Wyoming HoldCo is listed as the sole member
- Get registered agents in each state

### Step 4: Transfer Property Titles
- Quit-claim deed (or warranty deed) each property from JB's name into the corresponding operating LLC
- **Check mortgage due-on-sale clauses first** — if properties have existing mortgages, transferring to an LLC can technically trigger due-on-sale (most lenders don't enforce, but confirm with lender)
- Record deeds in county where property sits

### Step 5: Open Dedicated Bank Accounts
- Separate checking account for each property LLC
- All rent in, all expenses out through LLC accounts
- Wyoming HoldCo gets distributions from property LLCs

### Step 6: Annual Maintenance
- Keep WY LLC in good standing (annual report + fee)
- Keep property-state LLCs in good standing
- DO NOT commingle personal and LLC funds
- Keep up-to-date operating agreements

## IMPORTANT CAVEATS (Don't Skip These)

1. **Mortgage Due-on-Sale:** Most conventional mortgages have a due-on-sale clause. Consult with your lender BEFORE transferring. Most don't enforce for LLC transfers but confirm in writing.

2. **Insurance:** Update property insurance to list the LLC as the insured. Failure to do so can void coverage.

3. **Lender Approval:** If seeking future financing on these properties, some lenders won't lend to an LLC or require personal guarantee regardless.

4. **Land Trusts:** For states with weak property title privacy, consider a land trust (title in trust → WY LLC as beneficiary). Adds one more layer of anonymity. Ask Anderson Advisors about this.

5. **Do NOT collapse entities:** Don't put both properties in one LLC. Defeats the point of property-level isolation.
