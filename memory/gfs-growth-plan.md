# GFS $25M Growth Plan — Recurring Revenue Strategy

## The Core Thesis
GFS currently earns <10% from inspection, monitoring & maintenance (ITM).
Target: 25% of revenues from ITM within 3-5 years.
This is not just a revenue move — it's a valuation multiple re-rating.

## Why This Is the Right Play

### The Multiple Math
| Revenue Mix | Typical EBITDA Multiple |
|---|---|
| Pure construction/install | 3x–5x |
| Mixed (some ITM) | 5x–7x |
| 40%+ recurring ITM | 8x–10x |
| PE-backed platform, high ITM | 17x–20x |

**Current GFS at 4.5x exit (deal assumption) = ~$21M company at Year 5**
**Same EBITDA at 6.5x (25% recurring) = ~$30M company**
That's a $9M swing on the exit — from the same earnings, just a better mix.

### Monitoring MRR is valued differently
- Monitoring MRR: valued at **35x–45x monthly recurring revenue**
- Inspection ARR: valued at **2x–3.5x annual recurring revenue**
- These are additive to EBITDA multiples, not replacement

## Where the Money Comes From

### GFS's Natural Advantage
Every job GFS installs is a future service contract. They install the system — the building owner must inspect and maintain it by law. GFS has been walking away from that recurring revenue for 40 years.

**Addressable base:** Every completed job is a warm lead. Knox Street ($8.4M), QTS, DataBank, STACK — all of these buildings need annual ITM service.

## The Playbook: 10% → 25% ITM in 5 Years

### Phase 1 — Year 1: Lock the Installed Base (Low-hanging fruit)
**Goal:** Get contracts on every building GFS has ever touched

Actions:
1. **Audit the completed job list** — every job in the WIP history is a potential service contract
2. **Assign a dedicated Service Sales person** — this role doesn't exist today; must be created
3. **Contract every new install on close** — no project leaves without a service agreement signed
4. **Call existing construction clients** — "We installed your system. Let us inspect it."
5. **Lead with compliance** — buildings need annual NFPA 25/72 inspections by law; make it easy

Target: Convert 20-30% of historical install base to annual contracts in Year 1

### Phase 2 — Year 2-3: Dedicated Service Division
**Goal:** Build the recurring revenue engine as a separate P&L

Actions:
1. **Hire a Service Division Manager** — this person owns the ITM business
2. **Add 2-3 inspection techs** — dedicated to service, not construction
3. **Implement inspection software** (InspectPoint, ServiceTrade, or FieldEdge) — automated scheduling, deficiency tracking, digital reports, fast invoicing
4. **Tiered service agreements:**
   - Bronze: Annual inspection only
   - Silver: Semi-annual + monitoring
   - Gold: Quarterly + monitoring + priority response
5. **Monitoring partnerships** — contract with a UL-listed central monitoring station to white-label monitoring revenue without building own infrastructure
6. **Target property managers and building owners** — not GCs. GCs hand off the building. The owner is the recurring customer.

### Phase 3 — Year 3-5: Scale and M&A
**Goal:** Reach 25%+ ITM, become an acquisition target or platform

Actions:
1. **Acquire a small inspection-only company** — DFW has many 1-5 person fire inspection shops. Buy one for 2-3x revenue, instantly add their contract book.
2. **Expand territory** — Fort Worth, Arlington, Irving, Plano. Fire code is fire code everywhere.
3. **Target data center operators directly** — QTS, Equinix, CyrusOne all have DFW facilities GFS already works in. Propose multi-facility ITM contracts.
4. **Government/municipal contracts** — recurring, stable, low attrition

## Revenue Model: What 25% ITM Looks Like at $25M

| Segment | Revenue | Margin | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Construction/Install | $18.75M (75%) | 18-20% | Core business, stays strong |
| Inspection (ITM) | $4.5M (18%) | 35-45% | High margin, recurring |
| Monitoring | $1.75M (7%) | 50-60% | Highest margin, valued at MRR multiple |
| **Total** | **$25M** | **~24% blended** | |

At 25% ITM and $5M+ EBITDA → company values at 6.5x–8x → **$32M–$40M exit**
vs. pure construction at same EBITDA → 4.5x → **$22M**

**The recurring revenue play could add $10-18M to the exit price.**

## Operational Keys

### Technology Stack (Must-Have)
- **InspectPoint or ServiceTrade** — fire inspection software, automated scheduling, deficiency reports, customer portal
- **Connect to existing accounting** — avoid manual data entry
- **Mobile app for techs** — paperless inspections, photos, instant reports

### People (Critical Hires Year 1)
1. **Service Sales Rep** — hunter, owns contract acquisition
2. **Service Division Manager** (Year 2) — owns the P&L, not a tech
3. **2-3 Inspection Techs** — NICET certified, dedicated ITM (not pulled to install)

### Don't Cannibalize
Common mistake: pulling inspection techs to cover construction jobs. Guard against this. The service division needs its own dedicated headcount.
