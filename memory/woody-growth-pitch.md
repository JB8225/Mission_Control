# Woody Growth Pitch — "Here's What I'm Going to Do With What You Built"

## The Moment to Use This
After Woody says yes to the basic deal structure. Once he's nodding at $19M total value,
you open this up: "Woody, let me show you what I'm actually going to do with this company."

This is not a pitch deck. It's a conversation. 3 minutes max. Delivered with confidence.

## The Setup Line
"Woody, GFS is already a great business. 34% growth last year. $19M in revenue. 
Zero debt. You built something real. What I'm going to do is take what you built 
and turn it into something even bigger — and your 18% equity rides with every dollar of that."

## The Three Moves

**Move 1: Unlock the Hidden Revenue**
"Right now, less than 10% of GFS revenue comes from inspections and monitoring. 
Every building you've ever put a system in is required by law to get inspected every year. 
That's your customers — and they're paying someone else right now. 
We're going to fix that. I'm targeting 25% of revenue from recurring service within 3 years. 
That alone re-rates what this company is worth."

**Move 2: Grow to $25 Million**
"With a dedicated service team and a focused sales effort, GFS grows to $25M in revenue 
organically. DFW is one of the fastest-growing markets in the country. 
Data centers, hospitals, commercial builds — you're already in all of it. 
We just have to show up and sell the service side."

**Move 3: Strategic Acquisitions**
"There are dozens of small fire inspection shops across DFW — good operators, 
great customers, no succession plan. We buy 1 or 2 of them, absorb their contract books, 
and we're at $40-50M combined. That's when this becomes a platform, not just a company."

## The $19M Becomes the Floor Line
"Here's what that means for you, Woody. 

The deal we're talking about already gets you $19 million over 5 years. 
But your 18% equity doesn't stop at Year 5. 

If we hit $50M in revenue with 25% recurring — and I intend to — 
this company is worth $50-70 million at exit. 
Your 18% of that is $9-12 million. 

So the question isn't whether $19M is a good deal. 
It is. But the real question is: do you want to own 18% of a $70M company?
Because that's what we're building."

## The Close
"You spent 40 years building something great. 
I'm going to spend the next 10 making sure it becomes something extraordinary. 
And you're going to get paid twice — once at the deal, and again at the exit."
