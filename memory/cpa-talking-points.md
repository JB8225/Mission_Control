## ISSUE #1 — POCO Investments LLC Back Taxes

### What JB Needs to Tell the CPA

> "POCO Investments LLC was formed about 2 years ago. It has had zero financial transactions — no bank account opened, no revenue, no expenses, nothing moved through it. We just never filed the returns. I need you to file catch-up returns for 2023 and 2024 so this entity is clean before I apply for an SBA loan."

### Key Facts for CPA
- **Entity name:** POCO Investments LLC
- **State:** Texas (likely — confirm at formation)
- **Formation date:** ~2024 (approximately 2 years ago from March 2026)
- **Activity:** None — zero transactions, no bank account, no income, no expenses
- **Purpose when formed:** Intended to hold 6401 Poco Ct real estate (Fort Worth)
- **Actual use:** Property is still held under Lugap Consulting LLC — POCO never received title

### What the CPA Should Know

1. **Zero-activity returns** — With no income/expenses, these are simple returns. Federal: Form 1065 (partnership) or Form 1120-S (S-corp) or single-member disregarded entity (if JB is sole member, it flows to Schedule C/E). CPA needs to confirm entity type from formation docs.

2. **Late filing penalties** — IRS can assess $220/month/partner for late 1065 returns. But with zero activity and first offense, penalty abatement is often available. CPA should file with penalty abatement request (first-time abatement or reasonable cause).

3. **Texas state filing** — Texas has no income tax but requires an annual franchise tax report. With zero revenue, the No Tax Due report applies ($0 owed). Need to file 2023 and 2024 Texas franchise tax reports.

4. **SBA disclosure** — JB needs to be able to say: "POCO had no transactions — it was formed but never used. Catch-up returns are filed." That's the clean answer.

### Questions for CPA
- What entity type was POCO Investments LLC formed as? (Confirm from TX SOS formation docs)
- Is JB the sole member, or are there other members?
- What EIN was assigned? (Or was one ever obtained?)
- Has the Texas franchise tax account been set up for POCO?

### Cost Estimate for JB
- Two zero-activity LLC returns: $300–$600 per year typical CPA rate
- Texas franchise tax reports: often bundled or minimal charge
- Penalty abatement letter: some CPAs include this, some charge $150–$300 extra

## ISSUE #2 — Town Bluff Bad Debt Write-Off

### What JB Needs to Tell the CPA

> "About 18 months ago I lent $70,000 on a 90-day hard money note to a real estate borrower. The borrower defaulted. I later discovered the property has $40,000 in undisclosed liens that weren't disclosed at the time of the loan — possible lender fraud. I've been managing the property on PadSplit but it's basically breakeven. I want to write off the full $70,000 as a bad debt loss in my 2025 taxes. What do I need to document, and is there any fraud angle I should pursue?"

### Key Facts for CPA

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Amount lent | $70,000 |
| Loan type | Hard money, 90-day note |
| Date lent | ~2024 (approximately 18 months before March 2026 = ~Sept 2024) |
| Default date | ~2024 (within the 90-day term) |
| Recoverable amount | ~$0 (property encumbered by $40K in undisclosed liens) |
| Current income | ~$4,000/mo PadSplit revenue, ~$3,900–$4,800/mo expenses = breakeven/loss |
| Fraud flag | Undisclosed liens = potential lender/title fraud |
| Ace recommendation | Write off 2025; stop managing the property; document fraud for possible demand letter |

### CPA Guidance Needed On:

**1. Bad Debt Deductibility**
- Hard money loans are usually classified as non-business bad debts (IRC §166(d)) unless JB is in the business of lending
- **Non-business bad debt** = short-term capital loss (worst case — only $3,000/yr deductible against ordinary income unless offset by capital gains)
- **Business bad debt** = ordinary loss (full deduction in year of worthlessness)
- CPA should determine: Is JB's lending activity frequent enough to qualify as a "trade or business"? If JB has made 2+ hard money loans and this is a pattern, he may qualify. If this is a one-off, it's likely non-business.
- If non-business: full write-off in 2025 as short-term capital loss — useful if JB has any capital gains to offset

**2. Establishing Worthlessness in 2025**
- IRS requires the debt to be "worthless" in the year of the deduction
- JB should document: (a) demand letter sent to borrower, (b) no payments received, (c) $40K in senior liens = proceeds insufficient to repay JB's position, (d) decision to stop pursuing recovery
- Ask CPA: Should JB send a formal written demand letter before year-end 2025 to establish worthlessness for 2025 return? (Answer: yes, if not already done)

**3. Income from PadSplit Management**
- The PadSplit income ($4,000/mo) and expenses must be reported regardless of the write-off
- If JB doesn't own the property, this may be reported as ordinary income/expense on Schedule C or Schedule E (ask CPA — the characterization matters)
- Ace recommendation: **Stop managing this property** to eliminate ongoing complexity and close the loop cleanly

**4. Fraud Angle — Separate but Worth Exploring**
- $40,000 in undisclosed liens at the time of a hard money loan = possible title fraud or material misrepresentation
- CPA cannot advise on litigation but should note: **if JB recovers anything via a lawsuit or settlement in a later year, it would be taxable income** (recapture of the deducted bad debt)
- Ace recommendation: After the write-off is documented, have an attorney send a demand letter. Even if unrecoverable, it creates a paper trail and may trigger a settlement

**5. What Documentation to Gather**
- [ ] Original promissory note / loan agreement (the 90-day note)
- [ ] Evidence of the $70,000 transfer (wire/check confirmation)
- [ ] Borrower's default notice or documentation
- [ ] Title search or lien report showing the $40K in undisclosed liens
- [ ] Any written communication with the borrower post-default
- [ ] PadSplit income/expense records for the period JB managed it
- [ ] Any demand letters sent or attorney correspondence
