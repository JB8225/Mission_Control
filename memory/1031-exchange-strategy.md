## WHAT IS A 1031 EXCHANGE?

A Section 1031 exchange (named after IRS Code §1031) allows real estate investors to defer capital gains taxes when selling an investment property — as long as they reinvest the proceeds into a "like-kind" replacement property. No tax is paid at the time of the exchange. The gain is deferred until the replacement property is eventually sold (without another 1031).

**The core benefit for WeLoveLucy:** The Starbucks property has likely appreciated significantly since purchase. Selling without a 1031 would trigger capital gains tax (15–20% federal + Missouri state tax ~5.4%). A 1031 lets all partners roll their equity into a new property tax-free and keep compounding.

## THE RULES (what JB needs to know cold)

### 1. Like-Kind Property
- Both the relinquished property (Starbucks) and the replacement property must be "like-kind"
- For real estate: this is broadly interpreted — commercial NNN property qualifies as like-kind to another commercial NNN property
- **Starbucks NNN → Dollar General NNN → ✅ Qualifies**
- Starbucks NNN → raw land → ✅ Also qualifies (nearly any US real property to any other)
- Starbucks NNN → primary residence → ❌ Does NOT qualify

### 2. The 45-Day Identification Rule (CRITICAL)
- After closing the sale of the Starbucks property, JB has exactly **45 calendar days** to identify potential replacement properties in writing
- The identification must be submitted to the qualified intermediary in writing
- **No extensions. No exceptions.** Miss this deadline and the entire exchange fails.

**Identification rules (choose one):**
- **3-Property Rule:** Identify up to 3 properties of any value (most common, most flexible)
- **200% Rule:** Identify any number of properties as long as total FMV doesn't exceed 200% of the relinquished property value
- **95% Rule:** Identify any number of properties, but must actually close on 95% of the FMV identified

**Ace's recommendation:** Use the 3-Property Rule. Identify 3 strong replacement candidates before you even list the Starbucks property. Have them ready before the 45-day clock starts.

### 3. The 180-Day Closing Rule (CRITICAL)
- JB must close on the replacement property within **180 calendar days** of the sale of the Starbucks property
- The 180-day period runs concurrently with the 45-day identification period (they're not consecutive)
- If your tax return is due before the 180 days are up, you must file for an extension or the exchange may be jeopardized

### 4. Equal or Greater Value
- To defer ALL capital gains, the replacement property must be of **equal or greater value** than the relinquished property
- If JB buys a cheaper replacement, the difference ("boot") is taxable
- Example: Sell Starbucks for $1.5M, buy replacement for $1.3M → $200K of boot is taxable

### 5. Qualified Intermediary (QI) — Required
- JB cannot touch the sale proceeds. Period.
- A Qualified Intermediary (title company, exchange company, or attorney) must hold the funds
- JB must sign a QI agreement BEFORE the sale closes
- If JB receives the proceeds even briefly, the exchange is disqualified
- **Cost:** QI fees are typically $800–$1,500 per exchange — cheap for the tax savings involved

### 6. No Related Party Issues
- Cannot exchange into a property owned by a related party (family member, controlled entity) within 2 years of the exchange
- Unlikely to be an issue here

### The Tax Liability Without 1031
If WeLoveLucy sells the Starbucks property without a 1031:

| Scenario | Sale Price | Est. Capital Gain* | Federal Tax (20%) | MO State (5.4%) | Net Tax Hit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Short lease (7.5% cap) | ~$1.18M | ~$500K | ~$100K | ~$27K | ~$127K |
| New NNN lease (6% cap) | ~$1.48M | ~$700K | ~$140K | ~$38K | ~$178K |
| Upside (5.5% cap) | ~$1.61M | ~$800K | ~$160K | ~$43K | ~$203K |

*Estimated gain based on WeLoveLucy total capital account of ~$1.25M at purchase; actual basis depends on depreciation taken

**JB's 11% of that tax hit:** ~$14K–$22K out of pocket

**The group's total tax hit:** $127K–$203K — real money that disappears on a taxable sale

### The 1031 Advantage
- **Zero** federal or state tax at time of sale
- All proceeds reinvested into replacement property
- Replacement property generates more income (if buying at higher cap rate or larger asset)
- Partners' heirs receive a **step-up in basis** at death — meaning the deferred gain disappears entirely at inheritance. Zero tax, ever.

### What to Look For (the NNN replacement profile)
Target: $4.5M–$6M NNN property with investment-grade tenant, 10+ years remaining lease, absolute NNN structure

**Best replacement candidates for a 1031 from a Starbucks exit:**

| Tenant | Why It Works | Cap Rate Range | Typical Price |
|---|---|---|---|

| Walgreens | Investment grade, longer leases available | 5.0–5.75% | $4M–$9M |
| Tractor Supply | Strong rural/suburban performer | 5.5–6.5% | $2M–$5M |
| Starbucks (different location) | Already know the tenant, familiar structure | 5.0–5.75% | $2M–$4M |

**For the WeLoveLucy group specifically:**
If the Starbucks sells at ~$1.5M and there are 9 partners, each partner's proceeds range from ~$37K to ~$300K (based on their ownership %). Each partner would ideally roll into a proportionate share of a new property.

**Two structural options:**

**Option A — Single replacement property (group stays together)**
- WeLoveLucy LLC or a new LLC buys a single larger NNN property with all proceeds
- Partners maintain same ownership percentages
- JB manages the new property
- Cleaner, simpler, more buying power
- Risk: Diane and other conservative partners must agree to stay together

**Option B — DST (Delaware Statutory Trust)**
- Each partner receives their proceeds and individually invests in a DST
- DST is a passive investment vehicle that owns NNN real estate
- Partners don't need to agree — each can act independently
- No management required — truly passive (important for Diane)
- Minimum investment: typically $25K–$100K per investor
- **Best option for Diane specifically** — zero management, passive income, keeps her in real estate
