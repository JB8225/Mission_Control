## WHAT IS NICET?

**NICET** = National Institute for Certification in Engineering Technologies
- The gold standard certification for fire alarm and fire protection inspection technicians
- Recognized nationally; accepted (and sometimes required) by Texas AHJs
- Issued by NICET (nicet.org) — nonprofit credentialing body
- Four certification levels: I, II, III, IV (I = entry, IV = expert)

---

## DOES TEXAS REQUIRE NICET FOR FIRE INSPECTIONS?

**Short answer:** Texas does not mandate NICET at the state level, BUT:

1. **Individual AHJs (local fire marshals) may require it** — particularly in Dallas, Fort Worth, and major metro areas
2. **Large commercial clients increasingly require NICET-certified inspectors** for their own compliance documentation
3. **Insurance companies** may require NICET-certified inspection sign-off for certain policy types
4. **GFS's competitive positioning** — advertising NICET-certified technicians differentiates from smaller shops

**Texas State Fire Marshal Office (SFMO):**
- Texas requires fire alarm technicians to hold a **Texas Fire Alarm License (FAL)** issued by the SFMO
- The FAL requires: background check, exam, and continuing education
- NICET is NOT required for the FAL — but NICET-certified techs pass the FAL exam more easily
- Website: sfmo.texas.gov

**Key distinction:** Texas has its own licensing (FAL) that is mandatory. NICET is separate and voluntary at the state level, but increasingly required at the customer/AHJ level.

## THE FOUR NICET LEVELS

### Level I — Inspection & Testing of Fire Alarm Systems
- **Target:** Entry-level technicians, 0-2 years experience
- **Requirements:** 6 months documented work experience in fire alarm ITM
- **Exam:** Written exam covering NFPA 72, basic inspection procedures
- **Cost:** ~$120-$175 per exam attempt
- **Time to prepare:** 2-3 months of study (with job experience)
- **What it enables:** Can perform inspections under supervision; increasingly required by AHJs for co-sign

### Level II — Inspection & Testing of Fire Alarm Systems
- **Target:** Working technicians, 2+ years experience
- **Requirements:** 18 months documented experience + Level I or equivalent
- **Exam:** More advanced NFPA 72, troubleshooting, system design principles
- **Cost:** ~$120-$175 per exam attempt
- **What it enables:** Can sign off on inspection reports independently; most AHJs accept Level II
- **This is the target level for GFS SafeGuard inspection techs**

### Level III — Fire Alarm Systems
- **Target:** Senior technicians / project managers
- **Requirements:** 3+ years experience + Level II
- **What it enables:** Can oversee large projects, train Level I/II techs, sign complex systems

### Level IV — Fire Alarm Systems
- **Target:** Senior engineers / technical directors
- **Requirements:** Bachelor's degree equivalent + extensive experience
- **What it enables:** Engineering-level authority; rarely required for field inspection

## TEXAS FAL (FIRE ALARM LICENSE) — THE MANDATORY LICENSE

**What it is:** Texas-specific license required to install, service, inspect, and test fire alarm systems
**Issued by:** Texas State Fire Marshal's Office (SFMO)
**Requirement:** All GFS alarm inspection techs MUST have this

**License categories:**
- **FAL-E (Electrician):** For electrical work on fire alarm systems
- **FAL-I (Inspector):** For inspection and testing only — this is what SafeGuard techs need
- **FAL-C (Contractor):** For companies (GFS the company holds this)

**To get FAL-I:**
1. Background check
2. Complete SFMO-approved training (typically 40 hours)
3. Pass SFMO exam
4. Pay license fee (~$50-$75)
5. Renew every 2 years with continuing education (8 hours)

**Website:** sfmo.texas.gov/licensing/fire-alarm

## NICET FOR SPRINKLER/WATER-BASED INSPECTIONS

For SafeGuard Silver and Gold (which include sprinkler inspections per NFPA 25):

**NICET Water-Based Systems Inspection & Testing:**
- Level I: Entry inspection work
- Level II: Independent inspection sign-off
- Texas does NOT require NICET for sprinkler inspections, but some AHJs and property managers do

**Sprinkler contractors in Texas also need:**
- TSBPE (Texas State Board of Plumbing Examiners) licensing for fire sprinkler work (installation)
- Inspection-only work has lighter requirements — confirm with SFMO
