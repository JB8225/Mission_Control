## THE CORE FRAME (say this early, repeat it)

> *"Diane, I'm not here to change anything. I want to protect what we've already built — and make sure our families keep benefiting from it for years to come."*

Everything else builds on this. She's not anti-wealth. She's anti-risk and anti-complexity. Lead with safety, not upside.

## WHAT DIANE NEEDS TO HEAR (in order)

### 1. The problem — without a new lease, the building loses value automatically

> *"Right now, Starbucks's lease expires in September 2027. That's 18 months. Every day that passes, the building is worth less — not because anything went wrong, but because buyers pay based on how many years of guaranteed rent are left. At lease expiration, our building could be worth 20–25% less than it is today. That's not a maybe — that's how NNN real estate works."*

**The number:**
- Today's building value (short lease): **~$1.1M–$1.2M**
- With a new 10-year Starbucks lease signed: **~$1.5M–$1.6M**
- Value difference: **~$300K–$400K** — that's what's at stake by waiting

### 2. The good news — Starbucks wants to stay

> *"Here's the thing — Starbucks is already talking with us about renewal. They've been there 20 years. They don't want to move. That gives us leverage right now that we won't have in 12 months. If we move first and get the lease signed, we lock in the value before it erodes."*

### 3. Option A — Sign the new lease and sell

> *"Once the lease is signed, we list the building. Our broker finds a buyer who wants a 10-year Starbucks NNN. We close, everyone gets a check. Your 20% would be somewhere around $300K–$330K after closing costs. We pay the tax and that's the end of it."*

- 20% partner payout (sell at ~$1.5M): **~$300K–$330K**
- Timeline: 6–12 months after lease is signed
- Complexity: Low. Sign docs, wire arrives.

### 4. Option B — Sign the new lease and 1031 into a better property (Diane's money keeps working, zero tax)

> *"There's another option — and this one doesn't require paying a dime in taxes. We do a 1031 exchange. We sell the Starbucks building and roll the proceeds directly into a larger NNN property — think Dollar General, O'Reilly Auto, CVS. Your check actually goes UP from Day 1 because the new property would have a higher cap rate and longer lease. And when your children inherit your interest, they get what's called a step-up in basis — meaning all the tax disappears. Zero tax, ever."*

| | Stay Put (No Action) | Sell + Pay Tax | 1031 Exchange |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly income | Stays same, then drops | Lump sum, ends | Increases from Day 1 |
| Tax due | $0 now (deferred later) | 15–20% capital gains | $0 |
| Building value trend | Declining → 2027 cliff | Crystallized at signing | Preserved + growing |
| Your family's inheritance | Taxable (lower value) | Cash (taxable) | Step-up basis, zero tax |
| Effort required | None (for now) | Low | Moderate (JB handles it) |

> *"The 1031 is the best outcome for your family. But even if you just want to take your money and be done — the first step is the same: get the lease signed. That's what protects everyone."*

## DIANE'S LIKELY OBJECTIONS (and how to handle them)

### "I don't want debt."
> *"I completely understand. The new property can be purchased all-cash with 1031 proceeds — no mortgage required. In fact, a $4.5M property with no debt would generate more than the Starbucks building does today. You'd have more income, no debt, and a longer lease. But if you want zero complexity, Option A (just sell) works too."*

### "I don't want to deal with a new property."
> *"You wouldn't have to deal with anything. It's still NNN — the tenant pays everything. And I'd manage the LLC. Your role would be identical to what it is now: receive a check every month."*

### "What if the new tenant doesn't pay?"
> *"We'd target investment-grade tenants — Dollar General, O'Reilly Auto, CVS. Same category as Starbucks. These are publicly traded companies with billions in revenue. The credit risk is the same or better than what we have today."*

### "I just want to keep things the way they are."
> *"I hear you. Here's the problem: 'keeping things the way they are' actually means letting the building lose value every month as the lease gets shorter. What we're trying to do is lock in the value we already have. The simplest version is just getting the Starbucks lease renewed — that protects everything you've built with zero change to your situation."*

### "Can I think about it?"
> *"Of course. But I want you to know the window here is time-sensitive. Once Starbucks's internal planning cycle moves past 2027, our negotiating position gets weaker. The time to get their attention is now, while they're already talking to us. I just need a sense of whether you're open to moving forward — even just on the lease renewal. Everything else can wait."*

## THE ASK (keep it small)

Don't ask Diane to commit to a 1031 exchange or a sale. That's too much.

**The one ask:**
> *"Can I get everyone's blessing to engage a broker to help us negotiate the lease renewal with Starbucks? No obligation to sell — just let a professional handle the lease conversation so we don't leave money on the table. That's the only thing I'm asking right now."*

This is a yes/no on hiring a broker. It's low-risk, reversible, and moves the ball forward.
