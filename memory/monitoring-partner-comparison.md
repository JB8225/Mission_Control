## THE DECISION

GFS SafeGuard Silver and Gold tiers include 24/7 fire alarm monitoring. GFS will **white-label** a central monitoring station — not build one. This is the right call: building a UL-listed central station costs $500K–$2M+ and takes 18+ months to certify.

**What you're looking for in a partner:**
1. UL-listed central station (mandatory for commercial insurance compliance)
2. Fire-specific expertise (not just security/burglar alarm shops)
3. Commercial dealer/wholesale program with competitive per-account pricing
4. White-label capability — GFS brand on the reports, not their name
5. DFW-compatible (no geographic restrictions on account types)
6. Scalable — can handle 50 accounts or 500 without renegotiating

## ACE'S RECOMMENDATION

### **Start with: COPS Monitoring or Monitoring America**

**COPS Monitoring** is the strongest first call:
- Largest independent central station in North America
- UL-listed with 6 redundant monitoring centers
- Known for fire-specific commercial accounts — this is their core business
- Dealer program is well-established; clear terms and pricing
- "Your brand, our infrastructure" white-label model is exactly what GFS needs
- Pricing (~$7–$12/account/month) is moderate — you still clear $38–$65/account/month at $50–$75 customer price
- **Ask for:** COPS commercial dealer program. Tell them you're launching a subscription fire protection service for a $19M DFW company with 200+ commercial accounts targeting in Year 1.

**Monitoring America** as backup/comparison:
- If cost is the priority, they're the cheapest at ~$3.50–$5/account/month
- Means you clear ~$45–$70/account/month — best margin in the group
- Smaller operation than COPS, but solid for a company at GFS's scale
- Worth getting a quote from both and comparing actual dealer terms

**Avoid for now:**
- **Bold Group / Manitou** — enterprise complexity, high minimums, built for 500+ account platforms. Too big for Day 1.
- **Alarm.com** — primarily a security platform; fire is an add-on, not their strength. Good for hybrid fire/security if GFS ever adds security monitoring.
