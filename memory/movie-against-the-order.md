## Logline
A former first-round MLB draft pick who never found lasting security in the American game discovers his true calling managing in Mexican baseball — and must learn to coach his own son through the same disappointments he endured, as they chase a championship together against the franchise that once discarded him.

## Core Theme
*Purpose isn't granted by permission — it's proven through endurance.*

## Story Spine
- Opens: lemon tree scene, Culiacán — mother's dying lesson about peace and faith
- Crosses border as a kid daily (Tijuana ↔ Chula Vista)
- First-round pick, Texas Rangers — family in cowboy hats burst into his room
- Marries Carly (former Miss Texas)
- Never becomes "the guy" — 2002 World Series ring with Angels as platoon player
- Phone goes silent. MLB career ends without a moment.
- Hardest years — financial strain, Carly holding family together in Texas
- Carly gives him permission to go to Mexico — not generosity, survival
- Mexico: discovers coaching is his calling with the Tomateros de Culiacán
- Wins championships. Gets fired by a jealous owner despite success.
- Named Team Mexico manager for WBC — historic run, loses to Japan on late-inning HR
- Padres manager interview — second choice. Door closes again.
- Son Mateo: expects first round on draft night, slides to third round
- Ends up managing Charros de Jalisco against the Tomateros who fired him
- Mateo walks off the championship — against the team that discarded his father
- Father and son win together — not for validation, but dignity
- Epilogue: beach, kids playing baseball in the distance, quiet peace
